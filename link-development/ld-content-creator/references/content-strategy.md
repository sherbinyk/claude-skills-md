# Content Strategy Reference — Social Media Content Mix

> **When to use this file:** Read this when planning a content calendar, recommending post types for a campaign, or when the user asks what type of content to create. It defines the content mix framework, the four content categories, and platform-specific best practices.

---

## Why Content Mix Matters

Effective social media strategy uses a mix of content types to educate, inform, entertain, and inspire — not just promote. Research consistently shows that audiences disengage from feeds that are purely promotional.

**The 80/20 rule:** 80% of content should deliver value (education, insight, entertainment, inspiration). Only 20% should be direct promotion. This balance builds long-term audience trust and organic reach, making the 20% promotional content far more effective.

Engagement data by format (approximate benchmarks):
- Short-form video: 66% engagement rate advantage
- Images/static: 61%
- Live video: 37%
- Interactive content (polls, quizzes): 32%

## The Four Content Categories

Every piece of content should serve one primary purpose. Use this taxonomy when planning a content calendar or recommending post types.

### 1. Educational
Teaches the audience something new. Positions the brand as a trusted authority.

**Formats:** How-to guides, tips ("3 ways to..."), explainer carousels, infographics, FAQs, tutorials, industry glossaries.

**When to use for Link Development:** Product capability breakdowns, digital transformation best practices, "how government agencies can..." articles, comparison guides (e.g., on-premise vs. cloud).

**LinkedIn fit:** High. Thought leadership and educational content drive the strongest organic reach on LinkedIn for B2B brands.

### 2. Informational
Communicates specific, factual information about the brand, product, or industry.

**Formats:** Product announcements, company news, feature releases, event promotions, case study reveals, award announcements.

**When to use for Link Development:** New product launches, platform updates, event announcements (conferences, webinars), client wins or partnerships, award recognition.

**LinkedIn fit:** Medium. Works well for announcements, but should be framed around audience benefit, not just company news.

### 3. Entertaining
Showcases brand personality through humor, relatability, or cultural relevance.

**Formats:** Memes, GIFs, behind-the-scenes (BTS) content, team spotlights, day-in-the-life posts, relatable industry moments.

**When to use for Link Development:** Use sparingly and purposefully. B2B audiences respond to humanizing content that shows the people behind the brand. BTS content from events, team moments, and cultural snippets work well. Avoid forced humor.

**LinkedIn fit:** Medium. BTS and team culture content perform well; memes and GIFs should only be used if they align with brand voice and are relevant to the professional audience.

### 4. Persuasive / Conversion
Convinces the audience to take action. This is the 20% in the 80/20 rule.

**Formats:** Testimonials, case studies, product demos, direct CTAs, event registrations, free trial offers, limited-time promotions.

**When to use for Link Development:** Demo request campaigns, webinar registrations, whitepaper downloads, product pages, event sign-ups.

**LinkedIn fit:** High when paired with social proof. Case studies with client names, testimonials with job titles, and measurable outcomes perform well.

## Top Content Formats by Type

| Format | Category | Best Platforms | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Short-form video (Reels, Shorts) | Educational / Entertaining | Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn | Highest engagement rate; hook in first 3 seconds |
| Carousel posts | Educational / Informational | LinkedIn, Instagram | Swipeable = higher dwell time; great for step-by-step content |
| Static image + strong TOV | All types | LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook | TOV must work without reading the caption |
| Infographic | Educational | LinkedIn, Instagram, Pinterest | Data-dense content made visual; highly shareable |
| Behind-the-scenes (BTS) | Entertaining | Instagram Stories, LinkedIn | Humanizes the brand; shows team and culture |
| Poll / Quiz | Interactive / Engaging | LinkedIn, Instagram Stories | Boosts engagement; surfaces audience opinions |
| Live video / Streaming | Educational / Persuasive | LinkedIn Live, Instagram Live | Real-time Q&A; high authenticity signal |
| Long-form video / Podcast | Educational | LinkedIn, YouTube | Deep dives; builds authority over time |
| Quote / Inspirational post | Entertaining / Informational | LinkedIn, Instagram | Low-effort but high shareability if quote is genuinely relevant |
| User-Generated Content (UGC) | Persuasive | All | Social proof at its most authentic; re-share client content with credit |
| Case study / Testimonial | Persuasive | LinkedIn, Website | Must include measurable outcomes ("reduced processing time by 40%") |
| Industry news / Trend commentary | Informational / Educational | LinkedIn, X (Twitter) | Positions brand as a knowledgeable, up-to-date resource |

## Platform-Specific Best Practices

### LinkedIn (Primary B2B platform for Link Development)
- Thought leadership, industry insight, and educational content perform best
- Native documents (carousel PDFs uploaded directly) outperform external links
- Personal-tone posts from individual accounts outperform brand page posts
- Best days: Tuesday to Thursday, 8 to 10 AM or 5 to 6 PM local time
- Use polls for market research and engagement spikes
- Video auto-plays muted — captions are mandatory

### Instagram
- Visual-first; the image/video carries the message, the caption supports it
- Reels outperform static posts for reach
- Carousel posts generate the most saves and shares
- Stories are for real-time, ephemeral, and interactive content (polls, swipe-ups)
- Use 5 to 10 targeted hashtags, not 30 broad ones

### Facebook
- Mix of all content types; works well for community building and longer posts
- Video (native upload) gets highest organic reach
- Events and community content perform well for B2B
- Paid boost is often needed for organic reach

### TikTok / YouTube Shorts
- Hook in the first 1 to 3 seconds is everything
- Native, authentic content outperforms polished production
- Series content builds subscribers and return viewers

## Content Calendar Planning — Recommended Mix

When building a monthly content calendar for Link Development, apply this recommended mix as a starting point:

| Category | Share | Cadence (4 posts/week) |
|---|---|---|
| Educational | 40% | 6 to 7 posts/month |
| Informational | 25% | 4 to 5 posts/month |
| Entertaining / BTS | 15% | 2 to 3 posts/month |
| Persuasive / Conversion | 20% | 3 to 4 posts/month |

For LinkedIn specifically, skew toward Educational (50%) and reduce Entertaining to 10%. The audience comes for insight, not entertainment.

When the user asks for a quarterly or monthly content plan, use this mix as the distribution guide across the posts. Label the category for each post in the plan.
