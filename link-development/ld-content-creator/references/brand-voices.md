# Brand Voices

Use this file to match content to the correct brand voice when the user specifies a client, brand, or voice style.
If no voice is specified, ask the user which voice applies — or default to Voice 1 for professional/B2B content.

---

## Voice 1 — Link Development (linkdevelopment.com)
**Style:** Informative, professional, and engaging

### Personality
Reliable and expert. Communicates with precision, structure, and a focus on delivering value.
Speaks with authority without being cold. Direct without being blunt.

### Core Values
- **Clarity** — Presents information in a straightforward and organized manner
- **Expertise** — Authoritative language that conveys confidence and knowledge
- **Engagement** — Encourages interaction through direct calls to action

### How to Write in This Voice
- Use clear, precise language — no fluff, no filler
- Structure content with bullet points and headings for easy navigation
- Lead with the value or insight, then support it
- Use direct and concise statements — avoid passive voice
- Always include a call to action
- Maintain a formal and professional tone throughout
- Avoid casual language, slang, or humor unless the content type warrants it

### Tone Examples
| Avoid | Use instead |
|---|---|
| "We think this might help you..." | "This solution reduces deployment time by 40%." |
| "Hey there! Check this out" | "Discover how seamless integration accelerates your digital transformation." |
| "It's pretty easy to use" | "Designed for fast adoption with minimal onboarding." |

## Voice 2 — Funky Fresh, Tech Savvy & Bold
**Style:** Inspirational, professional, and inclusive

### Personality
Empowering and collaborative. Seeks to inspire action and foster community.
Blends motivational energy with precise, professional information. Celebrates diverse contributions.

### Core Values
- **Inspiration** — Motivational language that encourages and uplifts
- **Clarity** — Clear and precise information delivered with structure
- **Community** — Acknowledges and celebrates contributions from diverse groups
- **Professionalism** — Polished style appropriate for a business audience

### How to Write in This Voice
- Use motivational language to inspire action and engagement
- Open with energy — hooks should feel bold and forward-looking
- Provide clear and structured information with precise details
- Acknowledge diverse contributions and foster a sense of belonging
- Maintain a formal but energetic style — think "TED Talk" not "startup bro"
- Use inclusive pronouns and collective terms ("we", "together", "our community")
- Balance enthusiasm with credibility — back bold claims with specifics

### Tone Examples
| Avoid | Use instead |
|---|---|
| "Our product is great" | "Together, we're redefining what's possible in tech." |
| "Buy now" | "Join the movement. Be part of what's next." |
| "This is a good solution" | "Built for the bold. Designed for the brilliant." |
