# Article Research Reference

This file defines two research prompts for generating article topic ideas at Link Development. Always use live web search before generating topics — pull in recent developments, emerging research, and current trends to make sure the ideas are fresh and grounded in what's actually happening, not just what's in training data.

---

## Before You Start

Run web searches across the relevant domains to surface:
- Research papers or reports published in the last 3–6 months
- Emerging trends being discussed by analysts (Gartner, Forrester, McKinsey, WEF)
- Announcements from major tech companies (Microsoft, Google, OpenAI, Meta, etc.)
- Themes gaining traction in B2B or enterprise conversations

Use these findings to ground the topics you generate. The goal is ideas that feel timely and relevant, not generic.

## Output Notes

- Number all topics clearly (Topic 1 through Topic 5)
- Use the full structured format above for every topic — no shortcuts
- Each headline should be immediately usable as a blog post title (apply NYT-style title capitalisation)
- The trend support section should reference real things — if you searched and found a specific report or announcement, cite it
- Topics should feel distinct from each other — avoid clustering similar ideas
